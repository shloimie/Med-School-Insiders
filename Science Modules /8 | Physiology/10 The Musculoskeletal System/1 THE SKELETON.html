Components of the Musculoskeletal system:

  • Skeleton: Provides structural rigidity and support, calcium storage, and physical protection.
  • Muscles: Provide mobility, peripheral circulatory assistance, and thermoregulation (shivering reflex).

The Skeleton

The human skeleton is composed of varying types of bones. The following are given as examples of the variety of bones in the body – you do not need to study them in-depth. Note that skeletal structure is referred to as an endoskeleton because it is inside the body, but you may see the term exoskeleton in reference to organisms that have an exterior skeleton (Ex. scorpions and grasshoppers).

  • Long bones (ex. femur and humerus).
    • Contain bone marrow.
  • Short bones (ex. carpals and tarsals).
  • Flat bones (ex. skull bones, scapula).
  • Irregular bones (ex. vertebrae, sacrum).

Bones are connected by ligaments and joints.

  • Joint types:
    • Fibrous (Ex. skull sutures – composed of dense connective tissue (i.e. collagen)).
    • Cartilaginous (Ex. intervertebral disks – composed entirely of cartilage).
    • Synovial (Ex. knees – contain synovial fluid as a lubricant).
– Clinical Application –

Cartilage and ligaments are composed mostly of collagen and have very low levels of blood flow. This is part of the reason why injuries to the cartilage and ligaments generally take much longer to heal than injuries to muscle, skin, or other tissues.
  • Bone Structure:
    • Osteocytes are mature bone cells surrounded by bone matrix.
    • Bone Matrix: Composed of hydroxyapatite (an ionic compound composed of Ca2+, PO43-, OH-) and collagen fibers.
    • Osteoblasts secrete matrix to form bone.
      • Stimulated by calcitonin.
      • Osteocytes are osteoblasts that have matured and surrounded themselves in bone matrix.
    • Osteoclasts break down bone matrix and old osteocytes.
      • Stimulated by parathyroid hormone.
CHECK YOUR UNDERSTANDING

Tumors often retain the original function of their parent cells. What would be the expected symptoms of an individual who has a tumor of the parathyroid gland?

A parathyroid tumor would secrete parathyroid hormone, which stimulates osteoclasts. A patient with this condition would have a low density of calcium within their bones (and consequently, potential fractures), high blood [Ca2+], heart and CNS symptoms from ion imbalance, and possibly kidney stones from the excess Ca2+. In medical school, you'll learn the mnemonic "bones, groans, stones, and psychiatric overtones" for the symptoms of hypercalcemia.
